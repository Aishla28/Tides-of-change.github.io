HISTORY OF MIGRATION IN INDIA

Paleolithic Period (2.6 million years ago - 10,000 BCE)

Early humans inhabited various regions of the Indian subcontinent. Nomadic hunter-gatherer groups moved in search of food and resources.

Vedic Period (1500 BCE - 500 BCE)

The Indo-Aryans migrated into the Indian subcontinent from Central Asia.These migrations led to the composition of the Vedas and the development of Vedic culture.

Maurya and Gupta Empires (3rd century BCE - 6th century CE)

The Mauryan and Gupta empires facilitated trade and cultural exchanges with other parts of Asia.
Foreign scholars and merchants, such as those from Greece and China, visited and settled in India.

Islamic invasions and Mughal empire (7th- 19th centuries)

This period witnessed both voluntary and forced migrations of people of different religious and ethnic backgrounds. The Mughal Empire attracted artists, scholars, and traders from various parts of the world.

British Colonial Rule (18th - 20th centuries)

The British Raj saw the movement of Indian laborers as indentured servants to various parts of the British Empire.
Indian soldiers and laborers were also deployed overseas, especially during the two World Wars.

Partition of India (1947)

The partition of India and Pakistan led to one of the largest migrations in history, with millions of people crossing borders to their newly formed nations.
